About me

    I am a Mechanical Engineer currently working as a support engineer for a company that produces MRI machines for veterinary use. I am responsible for 1st and 2nd line support; this calls for quick reactions as we are normally dealing with customers that are scanning emergency patients and need fixes in very little time, not only I support external clients but also our own service engineers when a visit is needed.


    I have always been a tech enthusiast; I started building computers since the age of 10. My appreciation for technology pointed me to engineering and software development. Since the early days I have always been an autodidact, when I was 12 years old I started my own server for an online role-playing game that I enjoyed, this involved dealing with SQL, PHP, DHCP, virtual servers and firewalls. Today, people turn to me when it comes to advice for everything electronic, to which I happily offer my guidance. Being an autodidact immensely helped me as years later I needed those skills in my family’s business. I was responsible for implementing and deploying the main server (login, files, DNS, DHCP, VPN), 3D printing server, PABX, website, maintenance and tracking database, virtual servers, surveillance cameras/alarm, among others IT-related tasks. My current personal project is learning programming though game development, I am currently learning C# and Python on my free time as I believe this will be a great addition to my work skills and has already enabled me to proactively develop .NET software tools that improved response time on support cases reducing downtime for all systems.


    My first engineering internship was at Ford Motor Company, I started working as an intern in product development in body interior (Instrument Panel/Console). After my first couple of months at Ford I stated taking care of more complex tasks such as leading part design changes and part analysis. That is where I first started working with a CAD software. After almost 2 years I started working with 3D printing and became fascinated with it. I started studying Solidworks and in the last few years I gained excellent working knowledge in developing parts in CAD.


    Since then, I designed many custom parts for costumers and for myself, I have helped our BAJA SAE team at university by rebuilding the chassis and helping with FEA simulation. I became a volunteer tutor of Computer-aided design for a semester with regular weekly independent classes (6 hours/week). My final year Project (“An analysis of material robustness under the scope of quality and good practices in FDM PLA 3D print”) involved developing good skills on 3d printing, Solidworks, tensile strength and polymers. I have developed a comparative method between different parameters, relating them with surface quality and tensile strength to promote continuous improvement and optimization of the 3D printing process through Fused Deposition Modelling. Passed with distinction (10 out of 10).
